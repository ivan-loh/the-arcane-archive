Character Improvements — persistent stat upgrades

The thirteen CharacterImprovement store items are the most-frequent thing you’ll click in any run. They are hero-agnostic — every wizard sees the same pool, the bonuses apply on top of whichever character you selected, and they stack with starting modifiers, artifacts, and spell-specific rolls.

What "persistent" means here

Improvements are persistent within a single run: once picked from the in-run store, the stat bonus lasts until the run ends. They are not a meta-progression upgrade saved between runs (those live in the unlock / challenge system). Treat them as the rolling shop choices that shape your build from wave to wave.

All values below are sourced from data/characters.json → global_character_improvements, which mirrors the storeitems/characterimprovements/ resource folder in the game’s assets. The pool is intentionally tight: most picks land in one of four obvious S/A-tier improvements, and the difficulty of the run is the only real reason to deviate.

1. Improvements vs. Artifacts vs. Spell upgrades

Three different shop categories all look similar at a glance. Knowing which is which prevents you from over-picking one rail:

The three in-run upgrade rails
Rail Source data Applies to Stackable?
Character Improvement storeitems/characterimprovements/ Player’s own stat block Yes — multiple picks of same stat allowed (subject to per-stat cap, see §5)
Artifact artifacts/ Unique conditional / triggered effect Each artifact unique; never duplicates
Spell Upgrade spells/<Spell>/improvements/ One specific spell’s stat or variant Yes — spell stat improvements stack, variants do not

Character improvements are the baseline rail — they buff every spell you have equipped at once (when the buffed stat applies to that spell). A +15% Damage improvement buffs your Shotgun and your Boomerang and your Trail. A spell-specific +25% Damage roll buffs only the spell it’s attached to. For multi-spell builds this matters a lot.

3. Per-improvement detail

Each card lists the stat affected, the raw per-rarity values from the asset dump, and a one-line "when to take" verdict.

CharacterDamage

+% Damage to every spell

Adds directly to the shared Damage bracket (StatType 0). Same additive pool as starting modifiers and spell-specific damage rolls, so dilutes once stacked — but always positive value.

  • Common +5%
  • Rare +10%
  • Epic +15%
  • Legendary +20%

When to take: almost always among the top two picks. Diminishing returns only matter once the bracket passes ~150%.

CharacterFireRate

+% shots per second

Multiplies hit cadence linearly. Because FireRate is a separate aggregate from Damage, the two combine multiplicatively on final DPS — never skip the chance to balance them.

  • Common +6%
  • Rare +9%
  • Epic +12%
  • Legendary +18%

When to take: any time your existing FireRate bracket is below your Damage bracket. See damage-optimization §3.

CharacterCriticalChance

+pp chance to crit

Flat percentage points. Default is 10; the offer-cap is 80, meaning the improvement disappears from the pool once you hit 80. Pair with CriticalDamage.

  • Common +4
  • Rare +6
  • Epic +9
  • Legendary +12

When to take: until you cross ~40% chance, picking CritChance over CritDamage is correct at default 150 CritDamage. See the breakeven formula in damage-optimization.

CharacterCriticalDamage

+ to crit damage multiplier

Flat addition to CriticalDamage, which is itself a percentage (default 150). +50 takes you from 1+1.5=2.5× crits to 1+2.0=3.0× crits.

  • Common +10
  • Rare +20
  • Epic +35
  • Legendary +50

When to take: only after CritChance is above ~40%, or as a Legendary opportunistic pick.

CharacterMaxHealth

+ flat HP pool

The largest absolute numbers in the pool. Default MaxHealth is 600; a single Legendary takes you to 1000. Critical for low-HP heroes like Hatty (starts at 300).

  • Common +100
  • Rare +200
  • Epic +300
  • Legendary +400

When to take: top-pick for first 1–2 shops, especially on Hard+ difficulties. Skip if you’re running a Glass-Cannon build (low-HP artifacts reward staying fragile).

CharacterMovementSpeed

+% walk speed

Survivability scaling. Default 3.6 units/sec; each pick is additive percentage. Legendary tier ships disabled (value 0 in the data), so the practical max-tier pick is the Epic +18%.

  • Common +8%
  • Rare +12%
  • Epic +18%
  • Legendary — (disabled)

When to take: first 2–3 shops of any run; movement skill carries everything else. Pair with curse-heavy challenges that punish staying still.

CharacterArmor

+ flat damage subtraction

Subtracts the listed value from every incoming hit (clamped at zero). Synergises hard with the IncreaseDamagePerArmor artifact, which turns Armor into offence.

  • Common +16
  • Rare +26
  • Epic +36
  • Legendary +50

When to take: mandatory if you have the Damage-per-Armor artifact; otherwise pick once for the defensive baseline and move on.

CharacterDodge

+% chance to ignore a hit

On each incoming hit, rolls against your Dodge stat; successes negate the hit entirely. Hard offer-cap at 75 — the pool stops handing it to you past that.

  • Common +4
  • Rare +6
  • Epic +9
  • Legendary +12

When to take: excellent on Sun Mage (starts +7); good general-purpose defence anywhere else. Each point is worth more than 1% damage reduction because it dodges DoT triggers as well.

CharacterHealthRegeneration

+ HP regenerated per second

Flat HP per second. Default is 0; even Common +1 is felt between waves. Not included in the demo build.

  • Common +1
  • Rare +2
  • Epic +3
  • Legendary +4

When to take: sustain rail. Strong on Smithy (starts +1) and any healer-leaning composition; weak on Glass Cannon builds.

CharacterXPGain

+% experience per orb

Meta-stat — doesn’t damage anything itself, but compounds every other improvement by letting you level up faster and hit more shops. Offer-caps at 300.

  • Common +7%
  • Rare +12%
  • Epic +17%
  • Legendary +21%

When to take: early-run only. A +17% XPGain at wave 1 buys you maybe 3–4 extra shop visits over the run. The same pick on the last wave buys you nothing.

CharacterLuck

+ to rarity-roll bias

Biases shop and drop rarity rolls towards higher tiers. Hard offer-cap at 25 — well below most other caps, indicating the developers consider it strong.

  • Common +2
  • Rare +4
  • Epic +6
  • Legendary +8

When to take: early-run only, same logic as XPGain. Reginald starts +2 and pairs naturally.

CharacterPickUpRadius

+% XP and drop pickup range

Quality of life. Lets you collect orbs without retracing, which indirectly translates to more uptime on damage and fewer wasted orbs at wave transitions.

  • Common +8%
  • Rare +12%
  • Epic +18%
  • Legendary +24%

When to take: filler pick. Solid on Plant Mage (starts +20). Almost always lower-priority than a Damage or FireRate pick at the same rarity.

CharacterSize

+% spell area

Adds to the logarithmically-stacked Size bracket — nominal % is bigger than the felt %. See log-curve table. Common tier is disabled (value 0), so this only appears at rare and up.

  • Common — (disabled)
  • Rare +12%
  • Epic +15%
  • Legendary +18%

When to take: AOE / persistent-zone builds (Bell, Plant, EnergyZone, Meteor). One pick early is great; successive picks are crushed by the log curve.

4. Recommended pick order

A default-difficulty triage for when the store deals you a random spread. Higher tier always beats lower tier in the same row, but a Common high-priority pick generally beats a Legendary low-priority pick.

Difficulty modifier

On Hard / Apocalypse, swap items #1 and #2 down behind a second Damage / FireRate pick — you’ll die to outscaled waves long before you die to a single hit. On Easy, take Luck and XPGain greedily; you have all the survivability you need.

5. Interactions & caps

Stat-limit-for-offer (the offer cap)

Four improvements have a stat_limit_for_offer set in their asset:

  • CharacterCriticalChance — offer cap 80
  • CharacterDodge — offer cap 75
  • CharacterLuck — offer cap 25
  • CharacterXPGain — offer cap 300

Once your current value for that stat meets-or-exceeds the cap, the improvement is removed from the eligible pool for future stores. The other nine improvements have stat_limit_for_offer = -1 — uncapped.

Interaction with starting modifiers

Character starting modifiers are applied before the offer cap is checked. So a Sun Mage who starts +7 Dodge is already 7 points closer to the 75 cap than a baseline hero. Wizard King starts +10 CritChance and can comfortably reach the 80 cap with two Epic picks.

Interaction with artifacts

Character improvements feed the same stat pool that artifacts do. The combinations that change the calculus are:

  • #13 Damage per Armor + CharacterArmor → Armor becomes a Damage rail; promote CharacterArmor from priority #9 to priority #3 for that run.
  • #1 LessHP → MoveSpeed + #3 LessHP → XPGain + CharacterMaxHealth → conflict. Skip MaxHealth entirely on a low-HP build.
  • #8 Luck When Not Rerolling + CharacterLuck → comfortably saturates the Luck cap; skip additional Luck picks past Common.
  • #15 Elemental Damage per Infusion + CharacterDamage stack additively… sort of. The Element multiplier is outside the Damage bracket, so its value rises with your CharacterDamage picks. Both rails reinforce each other.

Interaction with spell-specific upgrades

Spell-store improvements (rolled per spell in the storeitems/improvements/ folder) cover most of the same stats. The crucial difference: spell-specific Damage rolls go into the same additive bracket as CharacterDamage for that spell, but other spells see only the CharacterDamage contribution. On a mono-spell build the two are interchangeable; on a 3+ spell build, prefer the CharacterDamage pick at equal rarity.

6. Cheat sheet

  • Default order: MaxHealth → MoveSpeed → Damage → FireRate → CritChance → XPGain (early) → CritDamage.
  • Always cap: Luck (25), CritChance (80), Dodge (75) — one or two picks each is enough.
  • Never max out: PickupRadius, HealthRegen — filler only.
  • Build-defining: Size for AOE comps, Armor with #13 artifact, MaxHealth for Hatty / low-HP heroes.
  • Disabled tiers in current build: MovementSpeed Legendary (0), Size Common (0).
  • Multi-spell tax: spell-specific Damage helps one spell; CharacterDamage helps all — prefer the global at equal rarity.
