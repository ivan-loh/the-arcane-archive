Damage Optimization — the complete guide

Everything you need to push numbers from "I’m winning" into "I’m bored, where are the enemies." The first half is mechanics — the actual formula your damage flows through. The second half is what to do with that formula: which stats to chase, in what order, under which archetype.

Empirical caveat

The Spell Brigade ships as an compiled code build — only class signatures and a handful of constants survive decompilation. Every multiplicative chain in this guide is reverse-engineered from those signatures plus the single embedded constant StatCalculator.Exponent = 0.5714286 (≈ 4/7). Where ordering or coefficients are inferred, you’ll see a callout. Real-world damage may differ from the numbers below by a few percent due to call paths we couldn’t trace (notably the resistance / corruption ordering inside RemoveHealth). Treat the math as 95% correct, not gospel.

New to the game? Read the guides hub first, then come back here once you’ve survived your first run. Already familiar? Jump to the cheat sheet at the end.

1. The damage formula

One screen, one diagram. Every hit your spells deal funnels through this exact pipeline, from the moment you press cast to the moment HP leaves an enemy bar: 

// Player spell → enemy HP, end-to-end

final = base_damage
        × (1 + Σ PercentAdd_damage%)            // Damage stat, additive
        × spell_specific_mult                // e.g. DealsMoreDamageTowardsCenter
        × 1.5                                // if hitbox = Weakspot
        × (1 + CriticalDamage / 100)          // if crit roll succeeds
        × (1 + ElementStrength_attacker%)    // Stat 30, attacker side
        × (1 + ElementWeakness_target%)      // Stat 27, target side (rare)
        × Π damage_multiplier_providers     // Acid debuff, Dark corruption, etc.
        − target.Armor                       // FLAT subtraction, clamped ≥ 0
        × 0.25                               // only if hitting an ally (friendly fire)

The single most important thing to internalize: the Damage stat (StatType 0) is one big additive pool. Every +X% Damage source — from your character’s base modifier, from spell improvements, from artifacts like Increase Damage Per Armor, from variants that read your party state — all collapse into the same 1 + Σ bracket. They do not multiply each other.

No log exponent on Damage

PercentAddLogarithmic (bracket raised to ^0.5714) applies to specific stats — Range (1), Size (6), Luck, Pickup Radius — not to the Damage stat. Size and Range still affect damage indirectly (more enemies hit, longer dwell), but no exponent ever enters the master damage formula above. See the canonical reference in mechanics / damage-system and per-stat calc types on the stats page.

Term-by-term breakdown

base_damage
The Damage entry in the spell’s base_stats. e.g. Arrow starts at 165, Shotgun at 300, Trail at 33.81. This is what every multiplier scales.
Σ PercentAdd_damage%
The whole additive bracket. Most damage sources land here: improvement pick +7% Damage, character bonus +6 Damage (Hatty), epic-tier roll, etc. Confidence: high — this is the canonical StatCalculator.CalculatePercentAddValue path.
spell_specific_mult
Hard-coded per-spell scalars and conditional spell-variant modifiers. Examples: Slash deals 1.5× on the front edge, Boomerang crits at apex, Trail does more damage at the end. Confidence: inferred — the dump shows variant classes but not their coefficients outside Parameters[].
weakspot × 1.5
Hard constant Hitbox.WeakspotDamageMultiplier = 1.5f. Hitboxes typed as Weakspot (boss heads, mostly) apply this multiplicatively, before crit. Confidence: high.
1 + CriticalDamage/100
On a successful crit roll (random(0,100) < CriticalChance), damage is multiplied by 1 + CriticalDamage/100. Default character crit damage is 150 ⇒ a crit deals 2.5× non-crit. Crit chance and crit damage are snapshotted on the PlayerSpellDamageInfo at cast time, not at hit time.
ElementStrength_attacker%
StatType 30. +X% damage with elemental (non-None) spells. Sources: the Elemental Damage per Infusion artifact, Star Mage’s +15% innate, certain late-game upgrades. Multiplied after the additive Damage bracket, so this is one of the few true multipliers in the formula — precious.
ElementWeakness_target%
StatType 27. Target-side multiplier. For PvE play this only meaningfully appears on Hatty (who carries +15 as a self-debuff) and on certain enemies tagged with element weaknesses. Effectively, enemies don’t carry this stat in normal play — treat it as 1.
Π damage_multiplier_providers
The per-target debuff product. Acid’s ResistanceReduction, Dark’s corruption multiplier, and any boss-script-injected multiplier ride this rail. Each provider returns its own scalar; the dump leaves the composition (sum vs product) ambiguous. Best-guess: product. Confidence: inferred.
− target.Armor
Flat subtraction, clamped at zero. This matters for player defence; bosses do not publicly expose Armor. Why flat-subtraction is the working hypothesis: (a) Armor is a Flat-calc stat, (b) the artifact Increase Damage Per Armor would be pointless if armor had built-in diminishing returns, (c) the genre’s ancestor (Vampire Survivors) uses flat subtraction. Confidence: inferred.
× 0.25 (friendly fire)
Hard constant FriendlyFireMultiplier = 0.25 applied when a player damages another player. AOE on top of teammates still does a quarter-strength hit. Confidence: high.

2. The logarithmic curve (Size, Range)

Two stats route through a different aggregator: PercentAddLogarithmic. That aggregator does not change the additive sum — it raises the result to 0.5714 (≈ 4/7). 

// PercentAddLogarithmic, applied to Size and Range
value = base × (1 + Σ mod%)0.5714

That exponent murders runaway stacking. Look at what nominal percentages actually buy you:

Nominal Σ vs. actual scalar after the ^0.5714 exponent

Practical reading

You can never out-grow your starting Size by more than about 4× (even +1000% nominal lands at ~3.94×), regardless of how much you stack. The first 50–100% of Size you pick up is almost all the value you’ll ever extract from the stat. After that, Size picks become low-priority unless your build needs the coverage (Bell, Plant, EnergyZone, Meteor).

Same curve applies to Range. The implication: on short-range spells like Slash or Trail, picking any Range improvement is a high-value first pick, but successive picks lose value quickly. On already-long-range spells like Bell or FallingStar, Range picks are nearly worthless.

3. Stat priority hierarchy

Every stat that can move your damage number, ranked by how much leverage one point/percent of it actually buys. Order is defaults — specific spells / artifacts will override individual rows. See archetypes for the override logic.

  1. S-tier — Damage (StatType 0)

    The direct multiplier on every spell hit. PercentAdd, so it sums with every other Damage source. Roughly speaking, an epic +15% Damage pick is worth between +8% and +15% final DPS, depending on how large your existing bracket is (diminishing returns are real — if you’re already at 1 + 2.00, adding 0.15 only moves the bracket from 3.00 → 3.15, a +5% final-damage bump).

    Stack ceiling: none, but each successive pick is worth less in relative DPS terms. Best sources: the generic Damage improvement (epic tier +7 at lvl 1 cycling 5/3/5/5); spell-specific Damage rolls in the spell’s store entry; artifacts Damage per Nearby Teammate, Damage per Revive, Damage per Armor.

  2. S-tier — FireRate (StatType 2)

    Multiplies hits per second linearly. Because it’s PercentAdd against the spell’s base FireRate — not against the Damage bracket — FireRate is a true second multiplier on DPS. +30% Damage and +30% FireRate together give roughly 1.30 × 1.30 = 1.69× DPS, while +60% Damage alone gives only 1.60×. Splitting matters.

    Stack ceiling: spell-dependent. Some spells’ per-cast animations cap effective rate; verify by watching your spell visibly hit a wall (animation locks). Best sources: generic FireRate improvement (epic +3 base), starting modifiers on Shotgun/Smg/Arrow (+12, +8, +12), artifact FireRate When Standing Still.

  3. A-tier — CriticalChance × CriticalDamage (12, 13)

    A multiplicative pair: expected per-hit damage gain is 1 + (CriticalChance/100) × (CriticalDamage/100). Default character starts at 10% / 150%, so baseline crits contribute 1 + 0.10 × 1.50 = 1.15× — a 15% effective DPS pad you already had.

    The breakeven question

    "Should I take +4 CritChance or +7 CritDamage when both are offered?" Compute the partial derivatives at your current values. With C = CriticalChance and D = CriticalDamage: 

    d(multiplier) / d C = D / 10000
d(multiplier) / d D = C / 10000

// +4 CritChance is better than +7 CritDamage when:
4 × D > 7 × C      ⇔      D / C > 1.75

    At default stats (C=10, D=150), D/C = 15 — wildly above 1.75, so CritChance is the right pick. The pivot only flips around D/C < 1.75, e.g. C=80, D=140. In practice, almost always pick CritChance until you’re above 40–50%, then rebalance.

    Best sources: CritChance improvement (epic +4 first, then +2/+2/+4/+4); CritDamage improvement (epic +7/+5/+6/+7/+7); artifacts CritDamage Standing Still (+100 conditional), CritDamage per Revive (+35 per), CritDamage per Common; Wizard King starts +10 CritChance, Flute Mage +5 CritDamage (but −3 CritChance).

  4. A-tier (spell-dependent) — Projectiles (StatType 20)

    Flat stat. Each extra projectile is functionally a +Damage multiplier the size of (new_count / old_count). On Shotgun (3 base projectiles), +1 projectile is 4/3 = 1.33× DPS — an enormous, multiplicative gain. On Meteor (4 base) it’s 5/4 = 1.25×. On a Beam (1 base), it’s a full doubling.

    The Projectiles stat is flat-aggregated, so stacks don’t suffer the Damage-bracket dilution, but the proportional gain naturally diminishes as your count grows (5→6 is only +20%). Best sources: the ExtraProjectile variant on Shotgun, Smg, Meteor, Beam; generic Projectiles improvement (rare in store).

  5. A-tier (spell-dependent) — Size (StatType 6)

    See section 2 for the math. AOE spells (Bell, Plant, EnergyZone, Meteor) treat Size as their primary damage multiplier — a larger area hits more enemies, and DamageTickRate spells hit each enemy more times.

    Stack ceiling: hard-felt around +200% nominal (1.87× effective). Past that, pick anything else. Best sources: generic SpellSize improvement (+4); most spells’ own starting modifiers already include +25% Size; the IncreaseSizeWhenStandingStill variant on Slash / Plant.

  6. B-tier — ElementStrengthMultiplier (StatType 30)

    A rare and pure-multiplicative damage source — it multiplies the post-additive damage by 1 + X%. Two sources only matter in the live game: Star Mage’s +15 baseline, and the Elemental Damage per Infusion artifact (+10 per infusion). With 4 infusions equipped that artifact alone reaches +40% — a true multiplicative 1.40× outside the additive bracket.

    Best sources: the infusion artifact — grab it the moment you commit to elemental builds.

  7. B-tier — Speed, Range, LifeTime (3, 1, 4)

    Indirect damage. Speed determines how reliably projectiles connect at a distance and how fast moving targets eat them. Range (log-stacked) widens the engagement envelope — great on melee (Slash, Boomerang), useless on already-long (FallingStar). LifeTime matters mostly for area persistence spells (Bell, Plant, EnergyZone, Trail) and beam dwell.

    Rule of thumb: one improvement each of these is worth chasing, more than one is not unless your spell archetype specifically wants it.

  8. C-tier — Luck (StatType 9)

    Meta-stat. Biases the rarity roll on store items and drops — better picks downstream, no immediate damage. Worth taking early when you have many picks ahead, never worth taking on a final shop. The artifact Luck When Not Rerolling pairs nicely with a no-reroll discipline.

  9. Defensive damage — Armor (StatType 14)

    Listed under damage because of one artifact: IncreaseDamagePerArmor converts Armor into Damage at a documented ratio of Parameters[1] = 0.25 per point (inferred: each Armor adds +0.25 to the Damage bracket, or +25% per 100 Armor). Stack AddArmorEqualToLevel + Armor per Objective and Armor becomes a damage stat. Without that artifact, Armor is purely defensive.

4. Build archetypes

Six tested skeletons. Each abuses a different multiplicative chain in the formula. Pick the one that matches the starter spell + first artifact the game hands you — trying to force an archetype against a poor opening is the most common reason builds stall.

Crit Stacker

Multiplies the multiplier

Push CriticalChance × CriticalDamage to where every hit averages 4–5× non-crit damage. The expected-damage formula 1 + (CC/100) × (CD/100) is unbounded — at 60% chance and 400% damage you’re at 1 + 0.60 × 4.0 = 3.4× average. Best on single-target heavy hitters where crits actually matter per shot.

single-target scaling

Spells

  • Rifle — 140 base, slow cadence, every shot wants to crit.
  • Slash — 296 base; the FrontEdge hitbox compounds with crit.
  • Boomerang — the BoomerangCriticalDamageOnApex variant is essentially a free +CritDamage on every apex hit.

Artifacts

  • HealOnCrit (#17) — turns offence into sustain; param 0.3 HP per crit.
  • CritDamage per Revive (#14) — +35 stacking; reach two revives, you’re at +70.
  • CritDamage Standing Still (#7) — +100 CritDamage while stationary; pairs with kiting walls.
  • CritDamage per Common (#20) — +1 per common picked; trivially stacks late.

Heroes

Wizard King (+10 CritChance), Flute Mage if you can keep the −3 CritChance trade under control.

Why it works: exploits the only term in the formula besides FireRate that’s a true multiplier sitting outside the additive Damage bracket.

Counter-play: very high HP bosses with damage caps; if all your damage comes from rare crit spikes, dry streaks feel awful.

Element Specialist

Multiplicative outside the bracket

Stack the same single-element infusion across every spell to feed IncreaseElementalDamagePerInfusionPerformed (#15) until your ElementStrengthMultiplier sits in the +40 to +60 range — a true 1.40–1.60× outside everything else. Best with Fire (DoT stacks) or Lightning (ricochet damage is itself elemental).

Fire Lightning scaling

Spells

  • Any 3+ spells, all infused with the same element. Repeat-infuse for FireDouble / LightningDouble to intensify the secondary effect.
  • Best vehicles: Orb, Arrow, Shotgun — high base damage per infusion event.

Artifacts

  • #15 is the keystone — everything else is gravy.
  • Pair with #20 CritDamage per Common for late-shop scaling.

Heroes

Star Mage brings a free +15; Moon Mage unlocks infusions earlier (SpellsAndInfusionsGrantedEarlier), turning more shops into stacking events.

Why it works: StatType 30 is one of two terms that bypass the additive Damage bracket. Every +10% there is "real" damage.

Glass Cannon

Low HP, high reward

Run with permanent low-HP risk to activate TheLessHPTheMoreXPGain (#3) (up to +40% XP) and TheLessHPTheMoreMoveSpeed (#1) (up to +21 MS). More XP = more picks = more Damage in the bracket. The MS keeps you alive at low HP.

risk scaling

Spells

  • Anything with kiting potential: Trail, Proximity, Orb, Bell.

Artifacts

  • #1 LessHP → MoveSpeed + #3 LessHP → XPGain together — ideally both.
  • Avoid healing artifacts; they ruin the trade.

Heroes

Hatty (starts −300 MaxHealth, so already low). Sun Mage with +7 Dodge to survive accidents.

Counter-play: single hits that exceed your shrunken HP buffer. Bullet-hell waves and chip damage kill you fast — don’t pick this in Squid Phase 3.

Tank-DPS

Armor IS damage

The hidden synergy: IncreaseDamagePerArmor (#13) adds +0.25 Damage per point of Armor. AddArmorEqualToPartyLevel (#2) is assumed to grant a 1:1 armor-to-party-level scaling (4 players × level 30 → 120 Armor → +30% Damage at the artifact-13 ratio). Stack #12 Armor per Objective (parameters confirm +5 Armor per objective) and you can pass +80 Damage from armor alone.

Assumption: artifact #2 ratio

The parameters_raw list for AddArmorEqualToPartyLevel in data/artifacts.json is empty. The 1:1 armor-to-level ratio used above is the most likely interpretation given the artifact name, but the coefficient is not exposed in the data dump — the in-game value could be a different constant. The example math is correct under that assumption; treat the absolute numbers as a working estimate, not a ground truth.

defence coop

Spells

  • Anything front-line: Slash, Trail, Proximity.
  • Bell with the BellZoneGivesArmor variant doubles down — the Bell’s aura itself feeds the artifact while standing in it.

Artifacts

  • #13 + #2 are the keystones.
  • #12 if you reliably hit objectives.

Heroes

Ludwig starts with +12 Armor and −10 MS — perfect baseline.

Swarm Clearer

AOE coverage × tick rate

Stack Size and Projectiles on persistent area / multi-hit spells so every tick lands on dozens of enemies at once. Damage per hit stays modest; hits per second across the screen go to four digits.

aoe wave-clear

Spells

  • Bell — 142 base × persistent zone × tick rate.
  • Plant — DamageTickRate 1.35, 5s LifeTime, IncreaseSizeAndDamageWithEveryAttack variant.
  • FallingStar — 13m base size, 201 damage, hits everything in a giant radius.
  • Meteor — 4 base projectiles + ExtraProjectile variant + ExtraLine.
  • EnergyZone — pure DamageTickRate spell, lives off Size + LifeTime.

Artifacts

  • Anything that doesn’t require single-target focus. #5 Damage per Nearby Teammate shines in co-op.

Heroes

Bell Mage, Plant Mage, Star Mage.

Boss Killer

Maximize per-hit; weakspot × crit

Single-target focused. Pair a high-base-damage spell with +CritChance, +CritDamage, and weakspot hits where possible — the hard 1.5× weakspot constant stacks multiplicatively with crit. Acid infusion is the secret sauce; the ResistanceReduction debuff multiplies all subsequent damage.

Acid single-target boss

Spells

  • Rifle (140 base, low cadence, AimAtHighestHP variant locks onto bosses).
  • Beam (104 base × 1.9 FireRate × tick stacking on a stationary target).
  • Slash (296 base + IncreaseSizeWhenStandingStill when you can dodge in melee).

Artifacts

  • The Crit-Stacker package (#17, #14, #7).
  • One Acid-infused spell to seed the resistance reduction; the rest can be any element.

5. Worked optimization examples

Concrete numbers, every multiplier shown. Use these as templates for your own builds — the structure is the same regardless of spell.

Example A — Arrow with Fire infusion + Damage-per-Armor

Setup: base Arrow (165 Damage, FireRate 0.404), 3 epic-tier Damage improvements (let’s assume the second and third picks are post-dilution and net +21% bracket each, so Σ Damage% = 87%), one ArrowFire infusion, and you’re running IncreaseDamagePerArmor + AddArmorEqualToLevel with party at 4×level 20 = 80 Armor. 

// Damage bracket assembly
Σ Damage% = 87      // 3 epic improvements
            + 20     // IncreaseDamagePerArmor: 80 armor × 0.25 = 20
            = 107%

spell_damage = 165 × (1 + 1.07) = 165 × 2.07 = 341.6

// Element strength: Star Mage hero passive +15 + 1 infusion
elem_str = 1 + 0.15 = 1.15

// No crit, no weakspot (regular trash hit)
final_per_hit = 341.6 × 1.15 = 392.8

Final per-arrow damage: ~393. Plus a Fire burn DOT ticking 3 times after.

Note how FireRate is missing from the calculation — Arrow’s starting modifier already adds +12 FireRate, so its effective cadence is 0.404 × 1.12 = 0.452 shots/sec, giving a raw DPS of 393 × 0.452 = 178 DPS. Now layer one +15% FireRate store pick: bracket goes from 1.12 to 1.27, cadence to 0.513, DPS to 202 — +13.5% from a single pick.

Example B — Slash crit build expected DPS

Setup: base Slash (296 Damage, FireRate 0.75). Build state: CriticalChance = 30, CriticalDamage = 250, Σ Damage% = 60. 

// 1) Base spell damage with additive Damage bracket
spell_damage = 296 × (1 + 0.60)
              = 296 × 1.60
              = 473.6

// 2) Per-hit expected damage with crits folded in
crit_mult = 1 + 2.50 = 3.50× // on crit

E[hit] = 473.6 × [(1 − 0.30) × 1 + 0.30 × 3.50]
       = 473.6 × [0.70 + 1.05]
       = 473.6 × 1.75
       = 828.8

// 3) DPS at base cadence (Slash starting +12% FireRate is in the bracket)
DPS = 828.8 × 0.75 = 621.6 per swing per second

Expected DPS: ~622 — ignoring weakspot bonus and area multi-hit. Slash’s 3m size means it routinely hits 4–6 enemies per swing in a wave, giving an effective screen-DPS of ~2500–3700.

Example C — Beam vs. Squid (185k HP) time-to-kill

Setup: Beam (104 Damage, 1.9 FireRate, 1 projectile). Build state: Σ Damage% = 80, Critical Chance = 40, Critical Damage = 200, no element-strength bonus, ExtraProjectile variant taken (so 2 concurrent beams). 

// Per-tick damage of one beam
tick_dmg = 104 × 1.80 = 187.2
crit_mult = 1 + 2.0 = 3.0×
E[tick] = 187.2 × [0.60 × 1 + 0.40 × 3.0]
        = 187.2 × 1.80
        = 337.0

// Ticks per second per beam = FireRate (Beam treats FR as ticks/sec)
single_DPS  = 337.0 × 1.9 = 640.3
dual_DPS    = 640.3 × 2   = 1280.6   // 2 beams

// Squid HP = 185000 (sum of all 3 phases as one health pool)
TTK = 185000 / 1280.6 = 144.5 seconds

Expected solo TTK on Squid: ~145 seconds with this build. Cut it to ~36s in a full 4-player party that’s all dishing damage; the boss fight is over in two phases.

Tweak to compare: replace ExtraProjectile with an Acid infusion stack. Acid’s ResistanceReduction debuff is inferred to add a ~25% damage-taken multiplier for the debuff duration. New single_DPS × 1.25 = 800.4, TTK = 231s — worse than the dual-beam variant. Lesson: variants that multiply your throughput (projectile count, FireRate) almost always beat target-side debuffs on bosses.

Example D — Shotgun ExtraProjectile vs. raw Damage

The classic dilemma every Shotgun player faces: the ExtraProjectile variant adds +1 to the base 3 (4 total). Is that better than an epic +15% Damage improvement? 

// Baseline: 3 projectiles, no extras
baseline_damage_per_volley = 3 × 300 = 900

// Option A: ExtraProjectile variant
A = 4 × 300 = 1200          // +33.3% vs baseline

// Option B: +15% Damage improvement (assume from 0 bracket)
B = 3 × 300 × 1.15 = 1035   // +15% vs baseline

// At 60% existing bracket: +15% becomes a much smaller bump
B' = 3 × 300 × (1 + 0.75) = 1575
B'' = 3 × 300 × (1 + 0.60) = 1440
delta = 1575 − 1440 = 135     // only +9.4% net

ExtraProjectile wins decisively at any non-trivial existing Damage bracket. Variants and Projectile picks are always preferred to additional +%Damage picks once you have ~60% in the bracket.

6. Pitfalls & traps

Trap: stacking Size past 200%

The log curve crushes you. +300% stacked Size only gives +121% effective coverage. Diversify into Damage, FireRate, or Projectiles once your spells visibly cover a comfortable area.

Trap: greedy Damage stacking

Going from +0% to +15% Damage adds 15% final damage. Going from +100% to +115% only adds 15/200 = 7.5% final. Once you’re past the first epic stack, FireRate, Projectiles, and CritChance all outperform another Damage pick per choice.

Trap: friendly fire 0.25×

That 0.25 multiplier still lets a high-DPS Bell or FallingStar erase a downed teammate in a single tick. Don’t drop AOE on top of allies you’re trying to revive. The FriendlyFireHealsInstead variant exists on six spells — take it in 4-player parties.

Trap: element mismatch on weakness-immune bosses

Most bosses don’t carry an ElementWeaknessMultiplier of any kind. Picking a damage profile because “Squid looks watery, let’s do Lightning” rarely pays off — what matters is your own ElementStrengthMultiplier (StatType 30), not the boss’s weakness. Element choice is about which DoT / chain effect compounds with your build, not RPS.

Trap: stat snapshot at cast time

CriticalChance and CriticalDamage are snapshotted into the PlayerSpellDamageInfo when the spell is cast, not when the projectile lands. This means: standing still to trigger CritDamage Standing Still and then casting a slow projectile means the projectile keeps the bonus even if you move while it’s in flight. Conversely, a Bell zone’s damage uses the stats at each tick (per-tick DamageInfo construction) — moving in and out of the standing-still bonus matters.

Inferred: stat caps

No hard cap is visible in the dump for any damage-relevant stat, but several effects clamp at the implementation level: IceEffect’s chill factor caps at 0.72 (72% slow), and OneShot-style damage hits a hard ceiling around 999,999. If your build is producing numbers in that range, you’ve already won — further damage stacking is cosmetic.

7. Late-game scaling

The role of ElementStrengthMultiplier at high upgrade tiers

At upgrade Tier 4 and beyond, you’ll see Damage picks landing later in the improvement pool while ElementStrengthMultiplier sources stay constant. Because StatType 30 is multiplicative outside the additive Damage bracket, its relative contribution grows the larger your Damage bracket gets: 

// Two scenarios, same +20% ElementStrength addition:

// Low-bracket build: 200 base, Σ Damage = 50
no_elem   = 200 × 1.5 = 300
with_20%  = 200 × 1.5 × 1.2 = 360     // +60 damage (+20%)

// High-bracket build: 200 base, Σ Damage = 250
no_elem   = 200 × 3.5 = 700
with_20%  = 200 × 3.5 × 1.2 = 840     // +140 damage (+20%)

Same +20%, double the absolute damage gained. Late-game, ElementStrengthMultiplier becomes the highest-leverage damage stat per point. This is why Star Mage’s innate +15 compounds so brutally in long runs.

The OneShot constant

Hard-coded at 999,999. Once a single hit can clear this number, you instakill any non-boss enemy regardless of armor, resistance, or current HP. With a Slash crit build at +200% Damage and +400% CritDamage, you can cross this threshold on weakspot crits as early as wave 18. Beyond that point, additional damage stacking is purely cosmetic (it does not, in fact, do anything to enemies that were already being one-shot).

Once you one-shot trash, pivot

Stop taking Damage picks. Start taking FireRate, Projectiles, Size, or defensive stats. At one-shot threshold, the only enemy that still matters is the boss — and bosses don’t care that you can deal 5M damage when only 999,999 lands.

Pick-order reminders

  • Round 1–3: grab one Damage epic and one FireRate. Lock in your spell’s defining variant. Take Luck if offered.
  • Round 4–7: commit to an archetype. Stack Crit (chance first), or infusions, or Armor synergy. Take the relevant artifact the moment it appears in the shop.
  • Round 8+: stop taking flat Damage. Hunt FireRate, Projectiles, ElementStrength, defensive stats. Avoid Size past +150%.
  • Boss arrival: if you have spare picks, pre-stand-still for #7 stacks; verify your CritChance is above 30%.
Final note

Damage in The Spell Brigade is a long, multiplicative chain where one weak link — a missing FireRate, an unused ElementStrength, an over-stacked Size — quietly halves your effective output. The mid-game players who win consistently aren’t the ones with the highest single number; they’re the ones whose chain has no zeros.
