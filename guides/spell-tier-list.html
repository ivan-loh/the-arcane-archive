Spell tier list — all 21 spells

A ranked opinion of every offensive spell in the game, based on the formula and stat-priority analysis from the Damage Optimization guide. Take this as one analyst’s reading of the data — the game has so much synergy depth that any spell can win a run with the right combination. The list ranks how easily a spell reaches its ceiling, not its hard maximum.

How to read this

SSS: pick instantly, build around it. S: strong in 2+ archetypes, no major weaknesses. A: shines in one archetype, viable in others. B: archetype-locked, weaker baseline. C: needs hero / artifact help to scale.

Methodology

Each spell is scored on three axes derived from its base stats and variant list:

  1. Throughput — raw DPS at base (Damage × FireRate × Projectiles), before any improvement. See the baseline DPS table at the bottom.
  2. Scaling — how much the spell’s own variants multiply its damage. ExtraProjectile, IncreaseSizeAndDamageWithEveryAttack, and BellZoneGivesArmor all push a spell up a tier.
  3. Archetype fit — does it cleanly slot into one of the six archetypes from the optimization guide? Versatility matters; one-archetype spells get docked.

Per-spell notes

SSS tier

Slash — SSS

Melee swing · AOE+Crit hybrid

296 base damage, the second-highest in the game, on a 3m frontal cone hitting multiple enemies per swing. Carries every archetype: Crit Stacker (huge per-hit base), Tank-DPS (Vampire Mage lifesteal), Swarm Clearer (3m AoE). The IncreaseSizeWhenStandingStill variant turns it into a screen-wide damage zone; ShootBackwards doubles hit-uptime. Base DPS 222.

Ceiling: highest sustained damage in the game when running Vampire Mage + #13 DmgPerArmor.

Shotgun — SSS

Projectile burst · ExtraProjectile abuser

300 base damage × 3 projectiles is gigantic, and the ExtraProjectile variant moves it to 4 (+33% damage) — far better than any +%Damage pick (see Example D). The Burst variant ramps cadence on standing-still. Pairs naturally with Campanelli’s kit. Base DPS 145, post-variant 194.

S tier

FallingStar — S

Sky-drop AoE

201 base, 0.87 FireRate, 15m base size — the biggest persistent AoE footprint in the game. Hits every enemy in a giant circle on impact. ExtraProjectile and LeaveDamageAreaAtEnd variants make it an elite-deleter. The natural vehicle for Star Mage’s +15 ElementStrength. Base DPS 175.

Bell — S

Persistent zone · armor synergy

142 damage applied over a 6.5s zone — raw DPS understates the spell because it ticks every overlapping enemy. The BellZoneGivesArmor variant is a hidden gem: it feeds #13 IncreaseDamagePerArmor while you stand in your own Bell. With IncreaseInSize + Size improvements you cover most of the screen. Base "DPS" 35 dramatically understates real throughput.

Orb — S

Auto-aim flak

170 damage × 1.15 FireRate = 196 base DPS, with an auto-targeting projectile that needs zero player input. Slots cleanly into Element Specialist (Reginald’s starter, and Reginald’s +2 Luck / +10 DropChance stacks shop quality fast).

Beam — S

Sustained line · boss killer

104 damage at 1.9 ticks/sec sustained on a 7.5m line. Eats bosses (see Example C). ExtraProjectile takes you from 1 to 2 beams — the largest multiplicative jump from a single variant in the game (+100% throughput). Base DPS 198.

A tier

Smg — A

High cadence · 4 base projectiles

118 × 1.455 × 4 = 686 raw projectile DPS. Sounds like SSS, but the projectiles are small (0.95 size) and short-lived (0.92s LifeTime); enemies at range eat very little. Excellent on Hatty for sheer throughput, weaker on heroes that can’t close range.

Meteor — A

Multi-projectile AoE

4 base projectiles, ExtraProjectile → 5, ExtraLine for additional spawn patterns, LeaveDamageAreaAtEnd for residual zones. 61 base damage feels low until you realize you’re hitting 5+ spots per cast. A consistent Swarm-Clearer pick.

Arrow — A

Heavy single-projectile

165 base, slow cadence buffed by +12% FireRate starting modifier. Excellent vehicle for crit stacking and infusions. ExplodeAtEnd variant makes it AoE on terminal range, which pairs beautifully with #15 ElementalDmg/Infusion. See Example A.

Plant — A

DamageTickRate persistent zone

118 damage, 5s LifeTime, ticks at 1.35s by default. The IncreaseSizeAndDamageWithEveryAttack variant is one of the few in-game compounding mechanics — size ramps over the spell’s lifetime, then resets. Strong swarm-clear, weak boss damage (single-target dilution). Plant Mage is the natural pairing.

Rifle — A

Single-target boss killer

140 base, low cadence, dedicated boss-killer with the AimAtHighestHP variant locking onto bosses. Lower wave-clear potential is why it’s A and not S. Wizard King’s +10 CritChance innate is purpose-built for this spell.

B tier

Trail — B

Movement-locked AoE

33.8 damage at 2.0/sec is the lowest base-DPS profile in the game, but it’s a per-step spell — the trail you leave damages anything in its path. The TrailSpawnsGuaranteedCrits variant elevates it significantly for Crit Stackers. Hard-locked to Key Mage’s kiting style; otherwise just OK.

Proximity — B

Aura around the player

50.7 damage × 1.8 FireRate sounds like A-tier numerically, but Proximity requires enemies to be right next to you, which is dangerous and limits use to specific heroes (notably Sun Mage with +7 Dodge, who treats it as a defensive aura).

Boomerang — B

Apex-crit gimmick

57 damage, two-way hit window. The BoomerangCriticalDamageOnApex variant is the only reason this is B and not C — it functionally guarantees crits on the far point of every throw, turning a mediocre spell into a fine Crit-Stacker vehicle. Without that variant: skip.

Mine — B

Stationary trap

134 damage on a 1.7m radius, 0.6/sec deploy rate. Trap spells are awkward because enemies have to walk into them — works in chokepoints, fails in open arenas. Solid filler when your other spells handle clear.

BombLauncher — B

Arc-shot AoE

149 base, 0.275/sec, 41 raw DPS but a sizeable explosion radius. Awkward aim (arc trajectory) and slow cadence keep it out of A; in the right hands it’s a respectable elite-clearer.

C tier

Laser — C

Slow persistent beam

128 damage at 0.196/sec = 25 raw DPS. Lower throughput than Beam with similar geometry and no comparable scaling variants. Skip when Beam is available.

Book — C

Floating projectile

140 damage at 0.2/sec = 28 DPS. Tiny 0.9m size, hard to hit moving enemies. Best as a Crit-Stacker secondary with the right hero, otherwise weak.

Summon — C

AI-controlled minion

107 damage at 0.21/sec = 23 DPS, plus the inherent AI unpredictability of summon targeting. Functional on Flute Mage (starter spell), forgettable elsewhere.

EnergyZone — C

Static damage zone

40 damage, 2m base size, 1.5s LifeTime — 10 raw DPS before tick-rate stacking. Needs enormous Size investment to function; that’s in tension with the log curve. Useable as a Swarm Clearer accent, never the carry.

Fence — C

Wall placement

47 damage at 0.08/sec = 3.76 raw DPS — the lowest throughput in the entire game by a wide margin. Fence is a utility spell (blocks enemy paths) more than a damage spell; rated on damage alone it’s a clear C, but in co-op crowd-control roles it has its place.

Baseline DPS reference

Raw Damage × FireRate × Projectiles at level 1, no improvements. This table is misleading on purpose — persistent zones (Bell, Plant, EnergyZone), multi-hit AoE (Slash, Trail, FallingStar), and DamageTickRate spells all vastly outperform their raw DPS number in practice. Use it as a starting reference, not a ranking.
