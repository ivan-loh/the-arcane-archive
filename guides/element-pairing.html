Element pairing — what the formula says

Five elements, two ways to stack them, twenty packed ElementType values. This guide cuts through the symbol soup with one rule: the only element decision that directly multiplies your damage is whether the spell carries an element at all. After that, you’re choosing between per-element effects, not damage scalars.

The key insight

From the mechanics spec: “If damageInfo.Element == None → return value unchanged (no elemental scaling).” Once a spell has any element, it picks up your ElementStrengthMultiplier bonus. The second infusion doesn’t double-stack the bonus — it changes the packed ElementType, which changes which per-element effects fire. The multiplier is the same.

The five base elements — what they do

Fire

Burn DoT

Per-hit, applies FireEffect: a burn DOT of Damage repeated NumberOfBurns times every SecondsBetweenBurns. Each burn tick can crit if CanCriticalHit is true (it usually is). With FireDouble, both the per-tick damage and the number of burns intensify.

Best on: high-base-damage single-hit spells (the DOT scales with the hit’s damage). Arrow, Shotgun, Slash.

Lightning

Ricochet chain

Per-hit, applies LightningEffect: chains NumberOfLightningRicochets jumps to nearby enemies, each dealing LightningDamageFactor × the original damage. LightningDouble increases both the count and the per-ricochet factor.

Best on: single-target high-damage spells in dense waves — the ricochets effectively convert single-target into AoE. Rifle, Beam, Orb.

Ice

Chill / slow

Per-hit, applies IceEffect: chill for ChillDuration seconds scaled by ChillDamageFactor. Clamps between 0.2 and 0.72 — hard cap at 72% slow. IceDouble pushes toward the hard freeze.

Best on: Glass-Cannon and Kiting builds. Not a damage element — a survivability element. Best utility infusion in the game for bullet-hell phases. Trail, Proximity, Bell.

Acid

Resistance reduction

Per-hit, applies AcidEffect: reduces the target’s damage resistance for AcidDuration seconds (effectively increases all subsequent damage they take). Sits on the target-side debuff rail rather than directly scaling attacker damage.

Best on: Boss Killer builds with a setup-and-kill rotation. Acid one spell, drop the heavy hitter through the debuff window. Synergy with co-op — the debuff applies to everyone’s damage.

Dark

Corruption

Per-hit, applies DarkEffect: adds corruption points scaling with CorruptionDamageFactor × damage. When corruption fills, the enemy becomes hostile to other enemies (Dark = turn the swarm against itself). The corrupted enemy’s outgoing damage routes through CorruptedPlayerSpellDamageInfo — attributed back to the corrupter.

Best on: Swarm Clearer builds in high-density waves. Lower direct damage than Fire/Lightning but the swarm-vs-swarm fights buy time and clear ranged enemies you couldn’t reach. Bell Mage + Dark Bell is a classic combo.

Single vs Double vs Dual

Single (Fire, Lightning, Ice, Acid, Dark)

One infusion applied to a non-elemental spell. Applies that element’s effect at baseline configuration. The first infusion is always the biggest leap — it turns on ElementStrengthMultiplier for the spell and unlocks the per-element effect.

Double (FireDouble, LightningDouble, …)

The same element infused a second time into an already-same-element spell. Intensifies the per-element effect (more burns, more ricochets, stronger chill, longer corruption, deeper resistance debuff). Does not stack the ElementStrengthMultiplier a second time — it’s still one element packed into the ElementType field.

When to double: only when the effect itself is doing meaningful work. Fire double doubles the DOT (worth it); Ice double pushes toward freeze (worth it on Glass Cannon). Dark double slightly increases corruption rate (marginal); Acid double extends the debuff window (situational).

Dual combo (FireLightning, AcidFire, DarkIce, …)

One spell carries two different elements. Both effects fire on each hit. There are 10 unique dual combos (every pair of the 5 base elements). The dual combo is one of the highest-leverage infusion decisions — you get two effects per hit at the same multiplier cost as one.

Rule of thumb

Default to dual combos. Two effects per hit for the same ElementStrengthMultiplier activation is strictly better in raw effect-density, unless you’re running an Element Specialist build that needs the matching infusion to stack #15 (which doesn’t care which element you pick — it counts infusion events, not infusion type).

Verdicts: per-archetype best infusions

Crit Stacker — Fire or FireLightning

Each burn tick can crit. A single Slash crit can fire 3 burn ticks, each rolling for crit independently — you’re multiplying your effective crit-event count by 3–5. Add Lightning for ricochet crits and you’ve doubled the crit surface.

Element Specialist — any single element, repeated

What matters is total infusion count for #15. Pick the element whose effect you like (Fire for DPS, Lightning for AoE) and stack same-element infusions across every spell you own. Each infusion event is +10 ElementStrength, regardless of which element it is.

Glass Cannon — Ice / FireIce

You need enemies to stop reaching you. Ice does that better than anything else; FireIce adds burn DPS while they’re stuck.

Tank-DPS — Fire

You stay alive, you stand in their face, you burn them down. The burn DOT is per-hit and the Tank-DPS build hits often via its inflated Damage bracket. Lightning ricochets are wasted on point-blank fights.

Swarm Clearer — Dark or DarkFire

Corruption turns wave size into a force multiplier. Once 4–5 enemies in a 30-enemy wave are corrupted, the wave eats itself in seconds. Add Fire for residual DOT cleanup.

Boss Killer — AcidFire or AcidLightning

Acid’s resistance debuff is the rare per-target multiplier that does affect single-target damage meaningfully. Pair with Fire (burn ticks scaling with debuff) or Lightning (ricochet damage scaling with debuff). On bosses with multiple hitboxes, AcidLightning is the better pick — ricochets count as hits, multi-stage targets eat more.

One spell per element rule

Even outside a strict Element Specialist build, try to make sure each spell in your loadout carries some element. The +10 stack from #15 applies per-infusion-event, so 4 spells with 4 different infusions is still +40 ElementStrength — a real 1.40× multiplier on every elemental hit.
