Best Spells Per Hero — build recommendations

A pairings guide. Every wizard starts with a fixed signature spell and a unique stat block; the question this guide answers is "what three companion spells most reward those starting conditions?" Reasoning is synthesized from each hero’s modifiers and unique abilities — not from official meta data.

How to read each card

Starting spell is fixed and always your first slot. Companion picks are three spells the in-run shop will eventually offer; the reasoning column explains why they amplify the hero’s opening modifiers. Avoid is one spell that actively works against the hero’s baseline — not forbidden, just sub-optimal.

Caveat

These are synthesized recommendations, not data-mined optimal-play results. The actual meta will shift as the developer rebalances stats. Use the cards as starting hypotheses; trust your own runs over this guide.

For the underlying math, see damage optimization. For stat definitions and rarity values, see stats and character improvements.

The fifteen heroes

Heroes are ordered by their in-game unlock cost, low-to-high. The first three are entry-level wizards; the last five are unlock-late heavy hitters.

Reginald

The starter — rarity-bias generalist

generalist loot-bias

Starting kit: +2 Luck, +10 DropChance, signature Orb (170 base damage, FireRate 1.15, 1m size). The Luck pushes shop rolls into higher rarities, so Reginald rewards spells with strong upgrade trees more than spells with high base numbers.

Companion picks

  • Shotgun — 300 base damage, 3 projectiles, gains the most from a single Epic/Legendary upgrade roll.
  • Arrow — 165 base + element-friendly; Luck-biased rolls hit the spell’s rich variant pool.
  • Meteor — 4 base projectiles, scales hard on the rare-tier ExtraProjectile variant.

Avoid

Fence — weak base numbers, thin upgrade tree; Reginald’s Luck has nothing to pull on.

Sun Mage

Small-target evasion bruiser

melee dodge

Starting kit: +7 Dodge, +7 TerrainSpeedMultiplier, −25 HitboxSizeMultiplier, signature Proximity (51 base, FireRate 1.80, 2.2m size). Smaller hitbox + dodge means Sun Mage can sit deep inside enemy packs — pair with close-range spells that reward being in the middle of the fight.

Companion picks

  • Slash — 296 base damage, 2m range; melee danger doesn’t apply when you can’t be hit.
  • EnergyZone — persistent area, 2m base size; you’ll stand in it indefinitely.
  • Trail — 33.81 dmg ticks but FireRate 2; rewards kiting circles around dense packs.

Avoid

Rifle — long-range single-target play wastes the small-hitbox advantage entirely.

Moon Mage

Early-infusion versatility

infusion xp

Starting kit: +3 XPGain, unique SpellsAndInfusionsGrantedEarlier, signature Boomerang (57 base, 0.53 FireRate). Earlier infusion access is the keystone — pair with spells that fundamentally change behaviour on infusion (Fire DoT, Lightning chain, Acid debuff).

Companion picks

  • Plant — persistent area + early Fire infusion turns the zone into a DoT carpet.
  • Summon — infused minions inherit the element; gets the buff one shop sooner.
  • Orb — high-FireRate vehicle for whichever infusion you commit to first.

Avoid

Fence — doesn’t benefit from earlier infusion timing; flat-damage spells waste the unique modifier.

Ludwig

Tank-DPS, walking armor stack

tank armor slow

Starting kit: +25 HitboxSizeMultiplier, +12 Armor, −10 MovementSpeed, unique IgnoresNegativeEffects, signature Laser (128 base, 0.196 FireRate, 1.84m size). Slow but immune to debuffs — pair with stationary or short-range spells that don’t need movement.

Companion picks

  • Beam — 104 base + 1.9 FireRate; channels best while standing still, which Ludwig does naturally.
  • Bell — persistent zone where you live; the BellZoneGivesArmor variant feeds the Damage-per-Armor rail.
  • Slash — 296 base; IgnoresNegativeEffects makes melee chip damage trivially safe.

Avoid

Boomerang — rewards mobility to retrieve apex hits; Ludwig’s −10 MS misses too many.

Smithy

Healer / sustain support

sustain support coop

Starting kit: +60 ReviveSpeed, +1 HealthRegeneration, +25 HealingMultiplier, signature Beam (104 base, 1.9 FireRate). Designed for co-op — the regen + healing modifiers reward staying alive through long fights.

Companion picks

  • Bell — healing aura variants and persistent zone synergise with the heal multiplier.
  • Plant — planted zones to stand in while channelling; pairs with HealthRegen uptime.
  • Orb — reliable autonomous damage while you focus on positioning teammates.

Avoid

Proximity — close-range play exposes you; Smithy’s value is staying alive, not pushing forward.

Wizard King

Crit-stacker single-target king

crit single-target boss

Starting kit: +10 CriticalChance (so 20 baseline), signature Rifle (140 base, 0.441 FireRate). Built for the Crit Stacker archetype — pair with high base-damage, low-cadence spells where each hit is worth crit-amplifying.

Companion picks

  • Slash — 296 base damage; the FrontEdge variant’s 1.5× multiplier stacks multiplicatively with crit.
  • Shotgun — 300 base per pellet; one Wizard King crit volley deletes most enemies.
  • Boomerang — the BoomerangCriticalDamageOnApex variant is essentially free +CritDamage on every apex.

Avoid

Trail — 33.81 base per tick; crits on a 34-damage tick add little, the value goes elsewhere.

Key Mage

XP-on-damage trail-runner

xp risk

Starting kit: +0.025 XPGainOnDamageTaken, signature Trail (33.81 base, 2.0 FireRate, 2.3m size). Rewards intentionally bleeding — pair with kite-friendly spells so you can spend HP for XP without dying.

Companion picks

  • Orb — autonomous damage while you focus on dodging-but-not-dodging-everything.
  • Bell — persistent zone with armor-grant variants to soften the HP loss.
  • Proximity — 1.80 FireRate close-range; rewards staying near enemies (where damage flows both ways).

Avoid

Rifle — long-range single-target play keeps you safe, which defeats Key Mage’s entire XPGainOnDamageTaken kit.

Bird Mage

Pickup & objective scout

scout objective

Starting kit: unique FastObjectiveDirectionPointer + ShowPickupDirectionPointers, signature Arrow (165 base, 0.404 FireRate). Tells you where everything is, which makes mobility-based spells far more efficient — you never walk the wrong way.

Companion picks

  • Boomerang — 57 base + 0.53 FireRate; you can pre-position throws toward objective markers.
  • FallingStar — 201 base + huge area; cue it on objective spawns you can pre-see.
  • Trail — rewards moving through objectives in straight lines, which Bird Mage knows where to draw.

Avoid

Fence — stationary deployment doesn’t benefit from scouting; the unique modifiers go to waste.

Hatty

Glass-cannon flat-Damage carrier

risk scaling

Starting kit: +6 Damage, −300 MaxHealth (300 total), +15 ElementWeaknessMultiplier (self-debuff), signature Smg (118 base, 1.455 FireRate). The +6 Damage is flat in the bracket — pair with fast-cadence spells to maximise hit count.

Companion picks

  • Arrow — 165 base + element friendly; rapid cadence stacks the +6 Damage on every shot.
  • Proximity — 1.80 FireRate is the highest in the game; +6 Damage applies per-tick.
  • Trail — 2.0 FireRate with persistent zone; constant tick coverage benefits the flat bonus.

Avoid

FallingStar — slow cadence (0.87 FireRate) wastes the +6-per-hit advantage, and Hatty’s 300 HP dies to a single mistake when she stands still aiming.

Campanelli

Mobility cannon, beam-on-spawn

mobility aoe

Starting kit: +10 MovementSpeed (so 13.6 vs default 3.6), unique HealthAbsorbingHugeSpawnBeam, signature Shotgun (300 base, 3 projectiles). Extreme MS rewards spells that depend on positioning — you can deliver any cone or melee spell where it matters most.

Companion picks

  • Boomerang — you can outrun the return arc and re-throw early; apex hits land more often.
  • Slash — 296 base, 2m range; the speed gets you into melee on demand and out before retaliation.
  • Trail — persistent path; high MS draws long damage lines across maps.

Avoid

Fence — stationary terrain spell; the mobility advantage is wasted standing next to it.

Star Mage

Element-specialist multiplier

element scaling

Starting kit: +15 ElementStrengthMultiplier, signature FallingStar (201 base, 0.87 FireRate, 13m area). The +15% is a true multiplier outside the Damage bracket — commit to a single element across every spell to compound it.

Companion picks

  • Arrow — 165 base, well-supported variant tree across Fire / Lightning / Ice infusions.
  • Shotgun — 300 base; each infused pellet doubles the elemental trigger count.
  • Orb — 170 base + 1.15 FireRate; reliable elemental application rate.

Avoid

Fence — the spell’s shipped behaviour leans toward neutral / physical; element multipliers find less to grab.

Vampire Mage

Self-sustaining bruiser

sustain melee

Starting kit: unique LifeStealOnSignatureSpell + CanReviveWhenDown, signature Slash (296 base, 0.75 FireRate, 3m size). Lifesteal procs only on Slash — so the signature does the sustaining, and companions cover everything Slash can’t (range, swarm).

Companion picks

  • Orb — covers the long range Slash misses while you stay in melee.
  • Bell — persistent zone keeps swarm pressure off so Slash uptime stays high.
  • Rifle — deletes priority targets (elites, casters) before they break your sustain loop.

Avoid

Trail — pulls you into mobile kite play, which competes with Slash uptime and breaks the lifesteal rhythm.

Plant Mage

Pickup-radius AOE specialist

aoe pickup

Starting kit: +20 HealthPotionAmountMultiplier, +20 PickupRadius (so 24 vs default 4), unique PickupAllXPOrbsWhenUsingMagnet, signature Plant (118.5 base, 0.27 FireRate, 3m base size). Huge pickup radius lets Plant Mage rotate between zones — pair with other persistent-area spells.

Companion picks

  • Bell — 142 base, 4m size; second persistent zone to chain between.
  • EnergyZone — pure DamageTickRate spell built to be camped in.
  • Meteor — 4 projectiles + AOE; carpets the area between your standing zones.

Avoid

Rifle — single-target sniping is the opposite of zone-camping carpet damage; the pickup radius advantage is wasted.

Flute Mage

Random-infusion summoner

summon variance

Starting kit: +5 CriticalDamage, −3 CriticalChance (7 baseline), unique RandomInfusionChance, signature Summon (107 base, 0.21 FireRate). The random infusion is great on multi-spell payloads — more spells means more chances per shop to roll the element you want.

Companion picks

  • Arrow — element-flexible; rolls well with whichever infusion the unique grants.
  • Orb — 1.15 FireRate triggers per-hit elemental effects often; variance averages out.
  • Meteor — multiple projectiles let elemental procs land on the same target multiple times.

Avoid

Rifle — low cadence means few elemental rolls; Flute Mage’s −3 CritChance hurts low-shot-count play even more.

Bell Mage

Return-damage close-quarters brawler

melee aoe return-damage

Starting kit: unique ReturnDamageWhenHit + ExplodeOnDeath, signature Bell (142 base, 0.25 FireRate, 4m size, persistent). Return-damage proc only fires at melee distance — pair with short-range spells that keep enemies close.

Companion picks

  • Slash — 296 base, 2m range; baits melee swings into your return-damage radius.
  • EnergyZone — persistent area you stand in; gathers enemies in punching range.
  • Proximity — the name says it; 2.2m base size + 1.80 FireRate while enemies pile in.

Avoid

FallingStar — pushes you into long-range play, which silences the ReturnDamageWhenHit kit.
