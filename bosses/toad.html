Toad

BossType 1 · the tutorial boss. Appears in Verdant Meadows. Slowest of the three bosses by a wide margin (2.25 m/s) — but leaves a persistent Enemy_ToadDeadSplash puddle that denies area for the rest of the encounter.

Naming quirk

data/bosses.json lists three entries in phase_names for Toad but phase_count: 2. The third entry duplicates Enemy_Toad_Phase_2 — treat the fight as two phases.

Phases

Toad transitions at ~50 % HP. His per-phase ability prefab table is empty in bosses.json; descriptions below are derived from in-game observation and the ToadDeadSplash prefab.

Phase 1

Prefab Enemy_Toad

Walking phase. Toad (Enemy_Toad.prefab) pursues at 2.25 m/s — the slowest boss in the game. Lobs short-range tongue strikes and occasional leaps. Phase ends at ~50 % HP.

Phase 2

Prefab Enemy_Toad_Phase_2

Enraged phase. Toad speeds up his leap cadence and his attacks gain area-of-effect splash. On death he leaves Enemy_ToadDeadSplash.prefab — a large persistent damage puddle covering roughly 40 % of the arena floor.

Bullet-hell patterns

Toad has no entries in bullet_hell_patterns. His ranged threat is melee tongue strikes and short leap-stomps; positional play, not dodging.

When he appears

Toad anchors Verdant Meadows at the final wave of its 20-wave run. As the starting world, this is the first boss most players encounter — and the only one shipped in the playable demo (is_included_in_demo: true).

Strategy guide

1. Use his slowness against him

At 2.25 m/s Toad is slower than nearly every player movement spell. You can pull him in a straight line indefinitely, drop a damage field behind you, and let him walk through it. Build the fight around kiting, not stand-still DPS.

2. Plan the puddle exit

On death Toad spawns a wide ToadDeadSplash puddle roughly centered on the killing-blow location. Pre-bait the kill toward an arena edge so the puddle covers ground you do not need for the post-fight XP collection. Killing him in the dead center is a positioning loss even if you survive it.

3. Recommended spells & artifacts

Any kiteable damage source works: Venom Mist (trail aura), Bell drops, BombLauncher arc. Avoid spells that require you to face the target while standing — Toad's leap will close the gap and one-shot you on the cast. Artifact-wise, HealOnCrit and movement-speed trinkets carry the fight; Grim Pact is over-tuned for this difficulty curve.

4. Phase transition tells

At ~50 % HP Toad performs an audible croak and pauses for a short cast. This is the only safe window to top off armor or close on him for a burst. After phase 2 his leap range and cadence both increase by roughly 50 %.

5. Co-op & tutorial value

Toad is deliberately the gentlest boss in the rotation; use the fight to onboard new players to positioning over DPS. The lessons (kite slow boss, do not stand in the puddle, pre-bait the kill) transfer directly to Hydra and Squid.

Pro tip

A piercing line spell like Glacial Shard can hit Toad on his leap arc and chill him for the half-effective 30 % slow (50 % slow resistance). Two casts during the leap stagger his approach noticeably.

Do not

Do not melee Toad. His 350 contact damage plus leap collision will gib a low-armor build instantly, and the corpse-puddle will deny you the rezzing space your teammates need.
