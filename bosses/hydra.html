Hydra

BossType 2 · the multi-headed summoner. Appears in Frozen Ancients / Astral Planes. Hydra is the only boss that self-heals: her healer adds (Enemy_Hydra_Healer.prefab) tick HP back onto her in a radius. Add-kill priority is the entire encounter.

Phases

Hydra's phase_data array is empty in bosses.json — meaning no per-phase ability prefab table is exposed. The three phase names are present, and we interpret them from in-game observation below.

Phase 1

Prefab Enemy_Hydra

Walking phase with no adds. Hydra (Enemy_Hydra.prefab) pursues at 2.75 m/s and relies on contact damage and basic projectile attacks. Use this window to position around the arena perimeter and chip ~1/3 of her 165k HP.

Phase 2

Prefab Enemy_Hydra_Phase_2

Healer phase. Hydra begins summoning Enemy_Hydra_Healer.prefab adds on a fixed cadence. Each healer in range restores boss HP every second. Burst-kill incoming healers before they reach her — they are fragile but plentiful.

Phase 3

Prefab Enemy_Hydra_Phase_3

Enrage phase. Spawn rate of healers increases and Hydra's movement aggression rises. The fight devolves into target-priority chaos: continuous AoE for healers, focused single-target on Hydra.

Data gap

Per-phase ability prefabs for Hydra are not exposed in data/bosses.json. Phase descriptions are derived from prefab names and in-game observation rather than asset data.

Bullet-hell patterns

Hydra does not appear in the bullet_hell_patterns registry — all six listed patterns belong to Squid. Hydra's ranged threat is conventional projectile attacks, not rotating walls.

When she appears

Hydra anchors Frozen Ancients (Crystal Frostlands) at the end of its 20-wave run and re-appears as a mid-encounter on the Astral Planes leaderboard variant. Run duration is ~1200 s, so expect the fight to open at the 18-minute mark of a clean run.

Strategy guide

1. Add control is the fight

Every Enemy_Hydra_Healer in range of Hydra restores HP per tick. Even one un-killed healer can stalemate a mid-tier DPS build. Run at least one wide AoE spell (Bell, Meteor, Bomb Launcher) and swap target priority every spawn — never tunnel on the boss while healers live.

2. Positioning & mobility

Unlike Squid, Hydra rewards perimeter kiting. Move counter- clockwise around her hitbox so healers spawn on the inside arc — this funnels them into your trailing AoE. The 350 contact damage applies if you cross her hitbox, so leave a buffer of at least 3 meters.

3. Recommended spells & artifacts

Pair one wide-AoE spell with one piercing single-target. Lightning archetypes shine because healers cluster (Chain Lightning bounces between them). For artifacts, HealOnCrit chains well off healer kills since their HP pool is small; IncreaseDamagePerArmor rewards stacking armor to survive accidental healer-clusters that include Hydra in their tag range.

4. Phase transition tells

Hydra's phase transitions are timed to the third and final phase_names entries, with healer spawn cadence stepping up at each. The visual tell is the central head changing posture — a low growl audio cue precedes the spawn-rate bump.

5. Co-op roles

Designate one player as healer sniper with high single-target piercing damage; the other 1–3 focus the boss. This split is the single largest predictor of clear time. A team that tunnels Hydra together can take 8+ minutes; a team that splits roles closes in under 4.

Do not

Do not chase Hydra past her healers — every healer left alive negates your damage output and prolongs the fight, increasing your exposure to the eventual enrage cadence.

Pro tip

A pre-placed Bell on the healer-spawn arc deletes freshly-summoned adds before they path to Hydra — effectively converting her summon ability into a free XP fountain.
