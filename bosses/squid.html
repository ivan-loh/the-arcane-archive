Squid

BossType 0 · the default boss and the only one with explicit bullet-hell patterns. Appears in Pyrestorm Pit (default). Three escalating phases of homing projectiles into rotating walls of fire.

Phases

Squid transitions on health thresholds rather than timers — at roughly two-thirds and one-third HP. Each transition is preceded by a brief stagger animation; bank movement cooldowns for it.

Phase 1

Prefab Enemy_Squid

Walking phase. Squid pursues at 3.0 m/s and lobs a single homing projectile from Enemy_Squid_HomingProjectile_Phase1.prefab every few seconds. Use this phase to chunk roughly the first third of the 185k HP bar — there is no bullet hell yet, just dodging the homing telegraph.

Phase 2

Prefab Enemy_Squid_Phase_2

Bullet-hell debut. Three patterns roll on a loop: NoRotationDoubleBurst (24 projectiles × 2 waves), RotateClockwise and RotateCounterClockwise (18 waves of 6 projectiles each). Projectile speed is 4.5 m/s, damage 100. Homing projectiles still spawn between patterns.

Phase 3

Prefab Enemy_Squid_Phase_3

Lethal phase. Pattern damage jumps to 150 and speed to 5.5 m/s. RotateClockwise and RotateCounterClockwise stretch to 24 waves at ~3× the rotation rate; the new Wavey pattern fires 24 waves of 4 projectiles each at 0.14s intervals on a sine path. No more homing — pure bullet hell.

Phase 3 math

At 24 waves × 6 projectiles × 150 damage each, a single fully-resolved RotateClockwise burst represents 21,600 potential damage. Even a 10 % hit-rate on you is lethal — pierce these spells with strafing motion, never absorb them.

When she appears

Squid is the default boss spawned at the run's terminal wave when no other world override applies. Cross-referencing data/meta.json > worlds, she anchors Pyrestorm Pit / Scorched Abyss at wave 20 (run duration ~1200 s).

The intro sequence (SquidBossIntroSequence.prefab) is long; you have time to pop a heal artifact, re-position to the arena center, and queue your highest-damage spell before her iframe drops.

Strategy guide

1. Positioning & mobility

Squid wants you in the arena center. Her bullet-hell patterns are nearly all radial — sitting on the edge gives you only one direction to dodge (inward), and the wall caps your strafe. From mid-arena you can pierce a pattern at its widest gap, then counter-rotate against her next ring. Move continuously; never stop to cast even for one tick.

2. Spell types that excel

Squid has 185k HP and 50 % slow resistance — favour raw single-target burst over crowd control. Void Lance and Glacial Shard excel because both pierce and travel fast enough to hit her while you strafe. Beam works once you can lock-on past her bullet curtains. Avoid pure-DoT builds: the fight ends before stacks mature.

3. Recommended artifacts

Sustain artifacts beat pure DPS here. HealOnCrit turns the 10-minute fight into a self-leech exchange when paired with a high crit spell. IncreaseDamagePerArmor rewards stacking armor into the 350-contact phase. Grim Pact is a damage trap in this fight — Squid's ambient damage already taxes survivability past comfort.

4. Phase transition tells

Squid plays a short stagger animation at ~66 % and ~33 % HP. The instant her body recoils, she resets all bullet-hell cooldowns — meaning the next pattern will fire immediately after the animation. Pre-strafe to the side during the stagger so you are already moving when the ring deploys.

5. Co-op roles

In a 4-player lobby, designate one player to tank homing projectiles by deliberately drawing them away from the damage core. Phase-2 homing projectiles will chase the closest target; a dedicated kiter halves DPS pressure on the rest of the team.

Pro tip

The Phase3_Wavey pattern is sine-modulated — its projectiles oscillate ±45° from the firing axis at 2 Hz. Step through a node (zero-crossing) rather than try to outrun the wave envelope.

Do not

Do not hug Squid. Her 350 contact damage plus a bullet-hell tick is an instant downstate at most armor values. Stay 6+ meters out even during burn windows.
